17:T1671,<h1 id="research-methodology"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#research-methodology">Research methodology</a></h1>
<p>BlackStory pins history to place. A publishable record needs claims, evidence,
provenance, and confidence, not assertion alone.</p>
<p>For the human case behind these rules, read <a href="./about.md">Why BlackStory</a>.</p>
<h2 id="principles"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#principles">Principles</a></h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Evidence before assertion.</strong> Public prose cites released evidence.</li>
<li><strong>Place is first-class.</strong> Locations carry precision; coarsened points are
never labeled as exact addresses.</li>
<li><strong>Confidence is visible.</strong> Uncertainty is glyph-encoded; color is never the
only signal.</li>
<li><strong>Living-person care.</strong> Unknown living status is treated as living; no public
residential addresses.</li>
<li><strong>Promotion gate.</strong> Research and LLM enrichment cannot publish. Human and
policy gates promote released projections only.</li>
<li><strong>Juxtaposition is not causation.</strong> Context indicators beside a law or place
are published measurements for comparison, not automated proof of policy
impact.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="pipeline-high-level"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#pipeline-high-level">Pipeline (high level)</a></h2>
<pre><code>discovery adapters
        ↓
 quarantine / triage
        ↓
  research cases
        ↓
claims · evidence · confidence lineage
        ↓
   promotion gate
        ↓
 released public projection
</code></pre>
<p>Operating detail lives in the repository under <code>docs/research/</code> and
<code>docs/methodology/</code>.</p>
<h2 id="landscape-entity-records-not-aggregator-pointers"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#landscape-entity-records-not-aggregator-pointers">Landscape: entity records, not aggregator pointers</a></h2>
<p>Most Black history portals excel at <strong>discovery</strong>: one search across hundreds of
institutions. They often link out to host sites rather than publishing <strong>entity
records</strong> with map pins, citations, and archived captures. When a holding
institution migrates its repository, those outbound links can break. Field
review of <a href="https://umbrasearch.org/">Umbra Search</a> in <em>The American Archivist</em>
(April 2026) documents that failure mode.</p>
<p>BlackStory's primary unit is different: a <strong>place-indexed entity record</strong>
(person, place, or event) with evidence you can still open when the origin site
moves. Wayback URLs or content-addressed capture pointers, plus confidence
grades and honest map precision.</p>
<p>Specialized corpora such as <a href="https://enslaved.org/">Enslaved.org</a> serve slavery
and freedom datasets at a scale and focus we do not replicate. Our lane is
<strong>general Black history</strong> across topics and eras, curated and growing. Not
complete. Not a substitute for domain-specific archives.</p>
<h2 id="juxtaposition-not-causation"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#juxtaposition-not-causation">Juxtaposition, not causation</a></h2>
<p>Users will ask what a law or policy <em>did</em> to Black communities in a place.
Academic-grade answers require evidence. Automated pipelines that emit "Policy Z
caused incarceration to rise X%" invent causation BlackStory must not publish.</p>
<p>Allowed shapes:</p>
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Shape</th>
<th>Allowed language</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Law / case entity with evidenced claims</td>
<td>Enacted, overturned, challenged</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Place-time indicator</td>
<td>In year Y, the published rate for Black residents in jurisdiction S was R (source…)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Derived ratio / gap</td>
<td>Black-White rate ratio was V in year Y, with formula and input ids</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Juxtaposition binding</td>
<td>Shown beside these indicators for the same jurisdiction and era, for context, not as proof of effect</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Causal claim</td>
<td>Only as a heritage claim with peer-reviewed or equivalent citation</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>When a binding is shown, the product includes a fixed disclaimer: context
indicators are published measurements from named custodians. Showing them with a
law or place does not establish that the law caused the indicator values.</p>
<h2 id="quality-before-breadth-claims"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#quality-before-breadth-claims">Quality before breadth claims</a></h2>
<p>We do not claim to index all Black history. Absence on the map is not proof
nothing happened. It may mean sources have not cleared the publish gate yet.</p>
<p>URL-backed citations are expected to carry <strong>archived captures</strong> before they
meet our public evidence standard. A capture completeness bar (95% of web
citations in the active release) gates when we market a queryable developer read
surface. That bar measures evidence readiness, not coverage of the field.</p>
<p>A machine-queryable public API or MCP layer is a <strong>planned target</strong>, not a
shipped product today. When it ships, it will expose published records only.</p>

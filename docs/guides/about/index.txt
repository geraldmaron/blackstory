17:T1576,<h1 id="why-blackstory"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#why-blackstory">Why BlackStory</a></h1>
<p>Black history is everywhere and somehow still easy to lose. A building gets renamed.
A newspaper digitizes and reshuffles its URLs. A local story lives in a basement
folder, a family scrapbook, or a grant-funded portal that stops getting updates.
Search can find a title. It rarely leaves you with a durable record you can trust
on a map.</p>
<p>BlackStory exists for that gap.</p>
<h2 id="the-feeling-we-are-building-toward"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-feeling-we-are-building-toward">The feeling we are building toward</a></h2>
<p>Open the map. Find a place. See a person, a school, a movement, an event pinned
with honest precision. Read a short claim. Follow the citation. Notice the
confidence grade. Notice when the pin is a block or a locality, not a pretend
street address. Leave knowing more than a headline, and knowing <em>how</em> we know it.</p>
<p>The product line is simple on purpose:</p>
<p><strong>History, pinned to place.</strong></p>
<p><strong>People. Places. Evidence. Context.</strong></p>
<p>That is not marketing garnish. It is the design brief. Place comes first.
Evidence comes before assertion. Context sits beside records without pretending
to be proof of cause. Pride without spectacle. Specific over sweeping. Invite,
do not lecture.</p>
<h2 id="what-most-portals-already-do-well"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#what-most-portals-already-do-well">What most portals already do well</a></h2>
<p>Discovery portals are excellent at breadth. They help you search across many
institutions and jump out to the host catalog. Specialized corpora such as
<a href="https://enslaved.org/">Enslaved.org</a> publish deep, domain-focused linked data
we do not try to replace.</p>
<p>Those projects matter. BlackStory is not a rebuke of them.</p>
<h2 id="the-empty-quadrant-we-are-aiming-at"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-empty-quadrant-we-are-aiming-at">The empty quadrant we are aiming at</a></h2>
<p>We want <strong>entity records</strong>: a person, place, or event you can hold in the
product. We want those records <strong>place-indexed</strong>, with map precision that tells
the truth. We want <strong>general Black history</strong> across eras and topics, curated and
growing, not a single corpus. And over time we want records that are
<strong>machine-queryable</strong> with stable fields, once quality gates say we are ready.</p>
<p>Most stacks occupy one or two of those axes. The overlap is thinner than it
should be. Aggregators widen access and also inherit link rot. When a holding
institution migrates its repository, outbound links can break while search
results still look fine. Field review of <a href="https://umbrasearch.org/">Umbra Search</a>
in <em>The American Archivist</em> (April 2026) documents that failure mode clearly.</p>
<p>Our answer is not "aggregators are bad." It is evidence pinned to place, with
archived capture pointers so a reader can still open the citation when the
origin site moves.</p>
<h2 id="what-we-refuse"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#what-we-refuse">What we refuse</a></h2>
<ul>
<li>Completeness theater. We do not claim to index all Black history. Absence on
the map is not proof nothing happened. It may mean sources have not cleared
the publish gate yet.</li>
<li>Sensational framing. Violence-adjacent records do not become crime-heat maps
or alarm-red spectacles.</li>
<li>Fake precision. A coarsened point is never labeled as an exact address.</li>
<li>Silent living-person risk. Unknown living status is treated as living. Public
residential addresses stay out.</li>
<li>Automated causation. Showing a law beside a demographic indicator is
juxtaposition for context, not a machine claim that the law caused the
number. Causal statements need separately evidenced claims.</li>
<li>LLM publish paths. Models can help draft and enrich behind the gate. They
cannot promote a public release.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="how-trust-is-supposed-to-work"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#how-trust-is-supposed-to-work">How trust is supposed to work</a></h2>
<p>A public page is not a research notebook. Readers see <strong>released projections</strong>:
immutable, reviewed slices of what we are willing to stand behind. Research,
quarantine, enrichment, and promotion stay private. Corrections land in intake,
not on the live canon.</p>
<p>Confidence stays visible. Color is never the only signal. Citations should carry
archived captures before they meet our public evidence bar. That bar measures
readiness of the evidence ledger, not coverage of the entire field.</p>
<h2 id="where-we-are-today"><a class="heading-anchor" href="#where-we-are-today">Where we are today</a></h2>
<p>The public product is live and growing. The catalog is on the order of thousands
of place-indexed entities, not a finished national inventory. Capture coverage,
developer query surfaces, and some cloud wiring are still catching up to the
architecture. This docs site is the orientation layer. The repository is the
source of truth.</p>
<p>If you are here to contribute, start with <a href="./install.md">Install and bootstrap</a>
and the <a href="./architecture.md">Architecture</a> guide. If you are here to understand
the research posture, read <a href="./methodology.md">Research methodology</a> next.</p>
